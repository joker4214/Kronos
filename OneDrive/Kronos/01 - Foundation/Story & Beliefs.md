# My Story & Beliefs

*Built from the founder interview — one question at a time. Living document, will keep growing.*

## Origin story

Before Dharma's Esthetic Design Center, Jason was working with another person on a Shopify store called **Dharmasemporium** (the ecommerce store behind the `@dharmasemporium` handle/brand).

Key realization: ecommerce stores are expensive and time-consuming to run. Jason has more money than time — and neither is unlimited.

The person he was working with had his own listed store, but it wasn't actually set up as a real commerce store — so he had no direct way to get paid, and was stuck going through other agencies that took a high cut. Jason's move: lease this person his (Jason's) real ecommerce store to work through, instead of that person relying on agency middlemen.

Result: Jason now has access to that person's ideas/expertise, plus two additional resources he met through Shopify — this is the seed of the current team (Mathew, Shuaib, Yusuf are the three team members named in the Brand Guide; need to confirm which one is "the guy" from the original story).

## Core belief / driver

> "I really do not want to wait until I am dead to retire."

This is the animating belief behind starting the agency.

**The goal, in his words:** building this to replace his current income and increase his overall financial stability — not explicitly framed (yet) as build-to-sell or build-to-scale-huge. Income replacement + stability first.

**Capacity:** Jason works a full-time, M–F, 9–5 job. Dharma's is built around evenings/weekends — this is the "more money than time" constraint in practice, and likely why the team (Mathew/Shuaib/Yusuf) exists: to cover execution he can't personally do during the day.

## Beliefs

Most agencies don't actually listen to their clients — this is the core thing Jason thinks the industry gets wrong. Ties directly to the "built around your brand" promise in the tagline/positioning: Dharma's differentiator isn't just AI + affordability, it's actually listening to what the store owner wants instead of pushing a cookie-cutter template.

**In practice:** it's a general mindset, not a formal intake step — listen to find out the client's actual needs and which service fits, because one size does not fit all customers. Every client gets matched to what they actually need rather than a default package.

## Who I serve

Ideal client: **pet store owners** (number one preference/affinity). Beyond that, pragmatically open to any client who is paying and ethical — not narrowly niched down, but pet ecommerce is the dream-client lane if given a choice.

## Team structure / lease arrangement

**Shuaib** is "the guy" from the origin story — Jason's own wording: "Shuaib is the lessor." The financial mechanics: Jason pays the store's monthly costs, and takes 11% of daily profits generated through it.
