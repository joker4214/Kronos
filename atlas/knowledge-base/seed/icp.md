# ICP — who Atlas targets

> This is the single most important seed file. Everything the fleet does — what it sources,
> how it scores, who it drafts to — keys off this. Edit it to your business. The example
> below is a worked default (an agency selling to online-store owners) — replace it.

## Who we sell to

- **Who they are:** Owners/operators of online stores (Shopify, Wix, WordPress/WooCommerce)
  doing real revenue but not yet a recognizable brand.
- **Size band:** Solo founder up to a small team. Enough revenue to have a budget, small
  enough that the owner is still the decision-maker.
- **Geography / language:** [e.g. US/UK/CA, English].

## The problem we solve for them

They have a working store but sales are inconsistent — the issue is
visibility/strategy/branding, not product quality. They can't justify a $5k/mo agency and
don't have time to DIY it well.

## Strong "act now" signals (these should score high)

- **Active ad spend with weak conversion** — money going out, sales not coming in. The
  sharpest signal.
- Recent store launch or redesign (in-motion, open to help).
- Publicly asking for help (forums, socials) with exactly this problem.
- Hiring for marketing/growth (budget + intent).
- A visible, fixable gap (broken checkout, no analytics, off-brand site).

## Disqualifiers (route: drop)

- Not actually an online store / wrong platform.
- Enterprise or already has a full in-house marketing team.
- No evidence they can act (no reachable decision-maker, no budget signal) **and** no pain.
- Explicitly asked not to be contacted.
